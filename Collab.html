// Organisation — Overview

What is WeWiki?

WeWiki is a documentation collective dedicated to cataloguing, researching, and publicly publishing findings on supernatural and paranormal phenomena. We exist because these things are real, they are dangerous, and most people have no idea they are there. Our mandate is simple: if it cannot be explained, it must be documented.

We build wiki-style websites — structured, readable, cross-referenced archives covering everything from anomalous spatial zones to entities that should not exist. Each site is built from survivor testimony, field reports, and first-hand accounts. Nothing on our sites is speculation. If it appears here, someone experienced it and lived to record it.

This archive — The Backrooms Wiki — is our most extensive active project. It covers one of the most well-documented and dangerous supernatural phenomena currently on record: a vast, non-Euclidean space accessible by accidental noclipping from reality. The information here has been gathered at great personal risk by individuals who entered and, against considerable odds, returned.

// How We Work — Methodology

📡
Collect

Testimonies, photographs, recovered notes, and field reports are submitted to our intake system and verified against known corroborating accounts before entering the archive.

🔬
Analyse

Accounts are cross-referenced for consistency. Anomalies are flagged. Contradictions are noted openly. We do not suppress inconvenient data — we publish it with context.

📄
Publish

Verified findings are formatted into structured wiki entries, categorised by type, danger level, and cross-links to related phenomena, entities, or levels.

🔄
Revise

Archives are living documents. New data overwrites old assumptions. Entries marked with revision notices indicate sections under active review due to conflicting new reports.

Jayden is the founder of WeWiki and the sole engineer behind every site the organisation has ever published. WeWiki exists because Jayden believed that this information deserved to be presented properly — not buried in forum posts, not scattered across disconnected threads, but structured, readable, and preserved.
